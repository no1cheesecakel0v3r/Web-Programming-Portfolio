Cast & Creative Team

Final Fantasy XV features talented voice actors and a dedicated creative team that brought the world of Eos to life. Here's a look at the people behind the characters and the development of the game.


Voice Cast

Ray Chase

Voice of Noctis Lucis Caelum (English)

Ray Chase brings depth and emotion to the character of Noctis, capturing his growth from a somewhat detached prince to a determined king. His performance helps anchor the emotional weight of the story, particularly in the game's more poignant moments.

Tatsuhisa Suzuki

Voice of Noctis Lucis Caelum (Japanese)

In the Japanese version, Tatsuhisa Suzuki portrays Noctis with nuance, balancing the character's royal demeanor with his more casual side when interacting with friends. His performance resonates with the character's inner conflict and ultimate acceptance of destiny.

Chris Parson

Voice of Gladiolus Amicitia (English)

Chris Parson's deep, commanding voice perfectly suits Gladiolus, bringing authority and intensity to the character. His performance particularly shines during Gladio's confrontations with Noctis, revealing the character's tough love approach to his duty.

Kenta Miyake

Voice of Gladiolus Amicitia (Japanese)

Kenta Miyake's powerful voice embodies Gladiolus's strength and determination in the Japanese version. His performance captures both the character's intimidating presence and his underlying loyalty to his friends and duty.

Adam Croasdell

Voice of Ignis Scientia (English)

Adam Croasdell delivers Ignis's refined accent and composed demeanor with precision. His performance evolves subtly as Ignis faces personal challenges, maintaining the character's dignity while revealing his vulnerability and unwavering dedication.

Mamoru Miyano

Voice of Ignis Scientia (Japanese)

Mamoru Miyano's elegant delivery suits Ignis's intellectual and proper character. His performance skillfully navigates the character's journey from composed advisor to someone fighting against overwhelming odds after losing his sight.

Robbie Daymond

Voice of Prompto Argentum (English)

Robbie Daymond captures Prompto's energetic and sometimes anxious personality with authenticity. His performance adds levity to the group dynamic while also conveying the character's insecurities and struggle for acceptance.

Tetsuya Kakihara

Voice of Prompto Argentum (Japanese)

Tetsuya Kakihara brings Prompto's cheerful exterior and hidden depths to life in the Japanese version. His performance conveys both the character's outward enthusiasm and his inner struggles with self-worth.

Amy Shiels

Voice of Lunafreya Nox Fleuret (English)

Amy Shiels brings grace and determination to Lunafreya, balancing the character's gentle nature with her unwavering resolve. Her performance emphasizes Luna's strength of will despite her relatively limited screen time.

Rina Kitagawa

Voice of Lunafreya Nox Fleuret (Japanese)

Rina Kitagawa portrays Lunafreya with ethereal dignity in the Japanese version. Her voice work reinforces the character's oracle status and spiritual connection while maintaining her humanity.

Darin De Paul

Voice of Ardyn Izunia (English)

Darin De Paul's mesmerizing performance as Ardyn stands out with its theatrical flair and underlying menace. His rich, melodious delivery perfectly captures the character's duplicitous nature and ancient bitterness.

Keiji Fujiwara

Voice of Ardyn Izunia (Japanese)

The late Keiji Fujiwara delivered a captivating performance as Ardyn in the Japanese version, balancing charm and malice with masterful subtlety. His work brings depth to one of the most complex antagonists in Final Fantasy history.

Creative Team

Hajime Tabata

Director

Taking over as director after the game's initial development as Final Fantasy Versus XIII under Tetsuya Nomura, Tabata restructured the project and guided it to completion as Final Fantasy XV. His vision for the game focused on the theme of brotherhood and the journey of Noctis and his friends.

Tabata's leadership was instrumental in delivering the game after nearly a decade of development. His dedication to connecting with fans through Active Time Reports and willingness to incorporate player feedback through patches and updates demonstrated his commitment to the Final Fantasy community.

Tetsuya Nomura

Original Director & Character Designer

Nomura's initial vision for the game as Final Fantasy Versus XIII shaped much of its world and characters. Though he moved on to direct other projects, including Kingdom Hearts III and Final Fantasy VII Remake, his character designs and core story concepts remained integral to Final Fantasy XV.

His distinctive artistic style is evident in the main characters' designs, particularly in the all-black attire that became iconic to the game's visual identity.

Yoko Shimomura

Music Composer

Yoko Shimomura composed the majority of Final Fantasy XV's stunning soundtrack, creating memorable themes that enhance the emotional impact of the story. Her work on tracks like "Somnus," "Apocalypsis Noctis," and "Valse di Fantastica" helped establish the game's musical identity.

Shimomura's compositions blend orchestral grandeur with intimate piano pieces, perfectly complementing both the epic scale of the adventure and the personal bonds between characters. Her soundtrack is widely regarded as one of the finest in the Final Fantasy series.

Kazushige Nojima

Scenario Writer

A veteran writer for the Final Fantasy series, Nojima crafted the main scenario for Final Fantasy XV, developing its themes of responsibility, sacrifice, and friendship. His writing emphasizes character relationships and emotional depth.

Nojima's work extends beyond the game itself, as he also wrote the script for "Kingsglaive: Final Fantasy XV," the companion film that depicts the fall of Insomnia concurrent with the game's opening.

Takeshi Nozue

Lead CG Director

Nozue served as the director for "Kingsglaive: Final Fantasy XV" and supervised the visual direction of the game's cinematic sequences. His expertise in CG filmmaking helped create some of the most visually stunning moments in the Final Fantasy series.

His work bridges the gap between gaming and film, bringing a cinematic quality to the game's presentation that enhances its storytelling impact.
